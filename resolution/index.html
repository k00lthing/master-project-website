Concept

Inspired by Hito Steuerl’s aspiration to be(come) invisible through occupying the low-resolution spaces (less-than-a pixel) this strategy utilises basic rectangular shape of a pixel (1:1 rectangle) to 'hide' the glyphs from the OCR software.

Barry Spencer’s Blank Typeface also explored the legibility of letterforms, devoid of recognisable characteristics.

Among being “invisible” and devoid of distinct letterforms characteristics the rectangular shape also bears the reference to censorship. Christine Rudi's master's thesis explored how information is restricted, banned, made unrecognisable, and inaccessible. To visualise these practices, she designed the font Index Mono, which combines the two extremes of censorship - blackening and disappearance. Thus, the variable font can be bolded beyond recognition or virtually disappear. Pixel Typewriter Font refers to pixelation in digital space as well as to the typewriter as a political instrument used to produce banned literature.
